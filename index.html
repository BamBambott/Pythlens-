What is Pyth Network?
Pyth Network is a first-party oracle that publishes financial market data on-chain. Unlike third-party oracles that aggregate data from public sources, Pyth receives price data directly from over 120 trading firms, market makers, and exchanges — the actual market participants.
Pull Oracle Model
Pyth uses a pull-based model where prices are published on Pythnet (a high-performance Solana appchain) and can be pulled on-demand to any blockchain. Applications only pay for the price updates they actually use, making it highly efficient.
Confidence Intervals
Every Pyth price comes with a confidence interval — a range of ± uncertainty. If BTC is $83,000 ± $50, that's very tight (0.06 bps) and indicates high agreement between publishers. A wide interval signals market uncertainty or low liquidity.
Exponential Representation
Pyth stores prices as integers with an exponent (like scientific notation). A price of 8300000000000 with expo -8 = $83,000.00000. This avoids floating point errors and allows precise representation of any price regardless of magnitude.
Pyth Entropy
Pyth Entropy provides on-chain verifiable randomness using a commit-reveal scheme. Users and the Pyth provider each commit a random value, then reveal them. The final random number is the XOR of both, meaning neither party can predict or manipulate the outcome.
